Why Rosebud?

In the 1941 movie classic Citizen Kane, Kane inherits a newspaper and is shocked to find that much news is filtered and censored. Driven by his belief in honest reporting, he establishes principles that ensure the truth reaches the people. His dying word “Rosebud” recalls a simpler, untainted time, symbolizing the fight against agendas and censorship.

Inspired by Kane’s determination, Rosebud World is built on the principles of integrity and free expression. We champion unfiltered, uncensored content and empower citizens to express their views without compromise.

Declaration of Principles

This service isn’t a news outlet—it’s a community platform where every voice matters. We embrace free speech and support articles from all perspectives while adhering to minimal legal requirements.

  • We do not censor opinions.
  • Articles can be anonymous or credited—your choice.
  • We celebrate freedom of expression without judgment of beliefs.
  • Freedom of speech is absolute and respected.
  • Posts must comply with our minimal legal requirements.
  • Rosebud is community owned and run with no special business interests.

Legal Rules

  • No posting of illegal images.
  • No direct calls for anyone to intentionally harm themselves or others.
  • No incitement to violence or forced acts.

Why register?

Registration is optional but we strongly recommend that you register even if you plan to blog entirely anonymously

If you submit a blog post without registering then your blog submission will be treated the same as if it was submitted by a registered user and will be indexed the same way.

However, someone else could later submit another blog post under the same blogger handle and we would have no way to know if that post came from you or not, and so it would also be checked and indexed.

This means that someone hostile to you could post fake posts under your blogger handle to discredit you. Registering ensures only you can submit posts under your handle without requiring any personal information in the registration process.

One an article has been submitted under an unregistered blogger handle we do not allow that handle to be registered later. This ensures that a hostile user cannot register using the handle of an unregistered user to effectively hijack his blogger handle.

How to Submit Articles

We currently accept submissions in one of two ways:

  1. Submission Form: Use our online submission form. This tool encrypts your article in your browser and uses the decentralized Matrix network to transfer your encrypted file. Our private service then retrieves, decrypts, and uploads your article to IPFS while indexing it. Because we do not operate any servers that directly connect to you, we cannot see your IP address. (Note: The Matrix network does use IP addresses for node connections, so if you’re concerned about that, please use a VPN or Tor.)
  2. IPFS Upload: Alternatively, you can load your article directly into IPFS provide us with the CID and an indication of whether you would like us to pin your original post or add it as a new post. This short guide provides an overview of IPFS and the benefits of each approach. Here you will find a page that optionally will encrypt your article prior to you uploading to IPFS should you want to maximise your privacy, and will allow you to submit your file CID to the Rosebud team via Matrix.

Once approved, articles are pinned on our IPFS node and listed here.

Note: To help keep content online and censorship-resistant, feel free to pin any approved articles on your own IPFS node.
